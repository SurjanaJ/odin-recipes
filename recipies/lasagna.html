Ratatoullie

Description

Ratatouille is a delicious and classic French vegetable stew that's not only flavorful but also healthy. It's made with a variety of colorful vegetables and is typically served as a side dish or a main course.

Ingredients

  • 1 eggplant (aubergine), cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 zucchinis, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 tomatoes, diced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 2 teaspoons dried rosemary
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for sautéing
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish (optional)

Steps:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Prepare the Eggplant: Place the eggplant cubes in a colander, sprinkle with salt, and let them sit for about 20-30 minutes. This helps remove excess moisture and bitterness. Afterward, rinse the eggplant cubes and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large oven-safe skillet or Dutch oven, heat a few tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onion and minced garlic. Sauté for about 2-3 minutes until they become fragrant and translucent.
  4. Add the Bell Peppers: Add the red and yellow bell pepper strips to the skillet and continue to sauté for another 3-4 minutes until they begin to soften.
  5. Add the Zucchini and Eggplant: Add the sliced zucchinis and eggplant cubes to the skillet. Continue to cook for another 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables start to brown and soften.
  6. Add Tomatoes and Tomato Paste: Stir in the diced tomatoes, tomato paste, dried thyme, and dried rosemary. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Mix everything well.
  7. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let the mixture simmer for about 10-15 minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld together, and the vegetables will become tender.
  8. Bake: If your skillet is oven-safe, you can place it directly in the preheated oven. Otherwise, transfer the mixture to a baking dish. Bake for about 20-25 minutes until the ratatouille is bubbling and the vegetables are tender.
  9. Garnish and Serve: Remove the ratatouille from the oven. If desired, garnish it with fresh basil leaves. Serve it hot as a side dish or a main course.
