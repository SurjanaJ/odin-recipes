Ponyo's Ramen

Description

Step into the enchanting world of Studio Ghibli's "Ponyo on the Cliff by the Sea," where the spirited and lovable character Ponyo revels in her love of ramen and ham. Although we can't recreate the whimsical sight of Ponyo devouring instant noodles straight from the package, we can embark on a culinary adventure inspired by her unwavering enthusiasm for the pleasures of food. Brace yourself for a journey into the heart of homemade ramen magic with this delightful recipe:

Ingredients

    For the Broth:
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon miso paste (white or red)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
    For the ramen
  • 2 packs of ramen noodles (discard the seasoning packets)
  • Sliced green onions (for garnish)
  • Sliced mushrooms (shiitake or cremini)
  • Sliced bamboo shoots (canned)
  • Sliced seaweed (nori)
  • Soft-boiled eggs (optional)
  • Slices of cooked ham (as a nod to Ponyo's love for ham)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a large pot, heat a little oil over medium heat and saut√© the minced garlic and ginger for about a minute until fragrant.Add the chicken or vegetable broth and bring it to a simmer. In a small bowl, mix the miso paste with a few tablespoons of the hot broth until it's smooth and well combined. Then, add it back to the pot. Stir in the soy sauce, and let the broth simmer for about 10-15 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  2. Prepare the Ramen Noodles: While the broth is simmering, cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. Assemble the Ramen: Divide the cooked ramen noodles among serving bowls. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles. Add your choice of toppings, such as sliced mushrooms, bamboo shoots, seaweed, soft-boiled eggs (cut in half), slices of ham, and sliced green onions.
  4. Serve: Serve your homemade Ponyo-inspired ramen hot and enjoy!

Feel free to customize your ramen with your favorite toppings or additional ingredients. This recipe is a fun way to pay homage to Ponyo's love of ramen and her passion for food.
